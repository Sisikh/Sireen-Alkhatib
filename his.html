Company History

Early Years (1800s-1900s)

These hotels catered to wealthy travelers and offered luxurious amenities like fine dining, comfortable rooms, and elegant lobbies.

Golden Age (1900s-1950s)

The early 20th century saw the rise of iconic 5-star hotels like the Ritz Paris (1898), the Plaza Hotel in New York City (1907), and the Fairmont Hotel in San Francisco (1907). These hotels were renowned for their opulence, fine dining, and exceptional service. The Golden Age of hotel luxury continued until the mid-20th century, when travel became more accessible to the masses.

Modern Era (1950s-Present)

The post-war era saw the rise of international hotel chains like Hilton (1946), Marriott (1957), and InterContinental (1953). These chains introduced standardized amenities and services across their properties, making luxury travel more accessible and consistent. The 1980s and 1990s saw the emergence of boutique hotels and design-driven luxury hotels, such as the Four Seasons Hotel in Boston (1970) and the Mandarin Oriental Hotel in Hong Kong (1963).

Contemporary Era (2000s-Present)

In recent years, luxury hotels have continued to evolve with new technologies, sustainable practices, and innovative design concepts. Some notable trends include: 1. **Wellness-focused hotels**: Properties like COMO Hotels & Resorts (2001) and The Retreat at Blue Lagoon Iceland (2017) emphasize wellness and self-care.
2. **Boutique hotels**: Unique, small-scale hotels like The Library Hotel in New York City (2006) and The Hoxton Hotel in London (2006) offer personalized experiences.
3. **Sustainable hospitality**: Hotels like The Brando in French Polynesia (2014) and The Gili Lankanfushi in Maldives (2005) prioritize eco-friendliness and environmental responsibility.
